About

When I'm not attending DevBootcamp or doing other programming work, I'm the owner and executive director of the Chicago Center for Literature and Photography, an organization that has been devoted since 2007 to showcasing and promoting the best of smart, extremely well-written indie literature and cutting-edge photography from both Chicago and around the world. We publish twelve original books a year, all of which can be downloaded for free from our website under a Creative Commons license (or purchased as traditional paperbacks from stores and Amazon); review 200 books a year at our blog by other writers; publish a free weekly electronic magazine (the CCLaP Weekender, six-time nominee for the Pushcart Prize); host a monthly reading series in Chicago; and occasionally host classes and workshops. I'm extremely proud of all of CCLaP's accomplishments, and plan on continuing to run it part-time even after starting my new career as a developer, so I hope you'll have a chance to stop by soon yourself and check it out.
